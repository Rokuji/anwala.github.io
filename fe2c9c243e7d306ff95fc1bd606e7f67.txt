<h2 class="article__deck">
'Wildest Dreams' hits No. 1 on the airplay chart, becoming the fifth leader from '1989.' Only Katy Perry's 'Teenage Dream' has generated more toppers – six – on the list.
</h2>
<p>Another week, another No. 1 for <a href="https://www.billboard.com/artist/371422/taylor-swift/chart">Taylor Swift.</a></p><p>Swift scores a fifth No. 1 from her album <em>1989</em> on <em>Billboard</em>'s radio-based <a href="https://www.billboard.com/charts/pop-songs">Pop Songs</a> airplay chart (dated Nov. 7), as "Wildest Dreams" lifts 2-1.</p><p>Prior to her latest leader on the Nielsen Music-measured Pop Songs chart, the first four singles from <em>1989</em> all reigned: "Shake It Off" (for two weeks); "Blank Space" (six); "Style" (three); and "Bad Blood," featuring Kendrick Lamar (five). ("Blood" also set the chart's record for the most plays in a week: 17,474 on the July 25 list.)</p><p><em>1989</em> is just the second album in the Pop Songs chart's 23-year history to yield at least five No. 1s, and the first such set churned out a record six: Katy Perry's <em>Teenage Dream</em>. The 2010 set bore the Pop Songs champs "California Gurls," featuring Snoop Dogg (seven weeks); the title track (four); "Firework" (four); "E.T.," featuring Kanye West (six); "Last Friday Night (T.G.I.F.)" (six); and "The One That Got Away" (one).</p><p align="center"><strong><a href="https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6738483/mtv-emas-2015-winners-justin-bieber-ed-sheeran-one-direction-taylor-swift">MTV EMAs 2015: Justin Bieber Triumphs as Ed Sheeran, One Direction and Taylor Swift Share Spoils</a></strong></p><p>(Rereleased as <em>Teenage Dream: The Complete Confection</em> in 2012, Perry added the four-week Pop Songs No. 1 "Wide Awake." That ballad and first single "Part of Me," which reached No. 3 on the chart, were not released on the original <em>Teenage Dream</em>.)</p><p>On the all-genre airplay, sales and streaming-based Billboard Hot 100, <em>Teenage Dream</em> is tied with Michael Jackson's <em>Bad</em> (1987-88) for the most No. 1s, five each, from a single set. <em>1989</em> has produced three Hot 100 No. 1s: "Shake," "Space" and "Blood." "Style" peaked at No. 6 and "Dreams" has also reached No. 6 so far.</p><p>"Dreams" is Swift's seventh No. 1 overall on Pop Songs (which ranks weekly plays on 170 reporting top 40 stations). She first led for a week in 2009 with "Love Story," from her album <em>Fearless</em>. (Perry and Rihanna pace all artists with 11 Pop Songs No. 1s apiece.)</p><p>Aiding airplay for "Dreams" at pop radio: R3hab's remix (released Oct. 15), which adds tempo to the original ethereal ballad version.</p><p align="center"><strong><a href="https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6730187/taylor-swift-1989-album-you-didnt-know">8 Things You Didn't Know About Taylor Swift's '1989'</a></strong></p><p>Last week, "Dreams" became the fifth No. 1 from <em>1989</em> on the Adult Pop Songs airplay chart, tying Perry's <em>Teenage Dream</em> for the <a href="https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/6731023/taylor-swift-ties-katy-perry-record-atop-adult-pop-songs-chart">most leaders from a single album</a> on the survey. This week, "Dreams" tops Adult Pop Songs for a second frame.</p><p>Check back later today to see where "Dreams" ranks on the latest Hot 100. All charts will update tomorrow (Oct. 27) on Billboard.com.</p>
