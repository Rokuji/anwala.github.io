<p>This family always ate conventional food because it was cheaper. That is until they got involved with <span style="color: #0000ff;"><a href="https://www.coop.se/Vart--ansvar/Ekoeffekten/The-Organic-Effect/Read-the-report-from-IVL/" target="">this study</a></span> conducted by the Swedish Environmental Research Institute IVL. The result surprised and disgusted everyone. Organic food was also recently found to be irrefutably higher in cancer-preventing antioxidants (<span style="color: #0000ff;"><a href="http://www.foodpolitics.com/wp-content/uploads/14-06-12-Final-Crops-Paper-BJN5552.pdf" target="">full study</a></span>). Considering the study presented in this video in addition to the higher antioxidant profile it&#8217;s about time for everyone who values their health to switch to an organic diet.<br />
The <span style="color: #000000;">increased use of pesticides</span> and the sky-rocketing disease rate in America is a huge correlation that cannot be ignored any longer. Of course it&#8217;s best not to depend on legislation to protect you and your family. The best step you can take after deciding to eat organically is to grow as much of your own food as possible. Even if you&#8217;ve never grown anything before it&#8217;s never too late to start and can be quite easy, enjoyable, and rewarding. Make sure you check into our <span style="color: #0000ff;"><a href="http://realfarmacy.com/grow-it-yourself/">Grow-it-yourself </a></span>category which is updated daily with gardening tips, tricks, and tutorials for both beginners and experienced gardeners alike.<br />
Second to growing your own <span style="color: #0000ff;"><a href="http://eatlocalgrown.com/how-it-works.html" target="" target="_blank">finding your nearest farmers&#8217; market</a></span> has become a must for the health-conscious consumer. Eat as seasonally and locally as possible, meet your farmer, and grow your own clean nutritious organic food. This is real healthcare and your family&#8217;s health is worth it.<br />
I&#8217;ll leave you with a quote from the <span style="color: #0000ff;"><a href="http://www.foodpolitics.com/wp-content/uploads/14-06-12-Final-Crops-Paper-BJN5552.pdf" target="">abstract</a></span> of what is considered to be the most comprehensive study comparing organic to conventional food published in the British Journal of Nutrition this past September. These solid findings didn&#8217;t receive as much press as they deserved. Show this to any friends or family that still eat conventional food.</p>

<p style="padding-left: 60px;"><strong><em>&#8220;In the present study, we carried out <span style="color: #ff0000;">meta-analyses based on 343 peer-reviewed publications</span> that indicate statistically <span style="color: #000000;">significant and meaningful differences</span> in composition between organic and non-organic crops/crop-based foods. Most importantly, the concentrations of a range of <span style="color: #ff0000;">antioxidants such as polyphenolics were found to be substantially higher in organic crops</span>/crop-based foods, with those of phenolic acids, flavanones, stilbenes, flavones, flavonols and anthocyanins being an estimated 19 (95 % CI 5, 33) %, 69 (95 % CI 13, 125) %, 28 (95 % CI 12, 44) %, 26 (95 % CI 3, 48) %, 50 (95 % CI 28, 72) % and 51 (95 % CI 17, 86) % higher, respectively. Many of these compounds have previously been linked to a <span style="color: #ff0000;">reduced risk of chronic diseases, including CVD and neurodegenerative diseases and certain cancers</span>, in dietary intervention and epidemiological studies. Additionally, the frequency of occurrence of <span style="color: #ff0000;">pesticide residues was found to be four times higher in conventional crops</span>, which also contained significantly <span style="color: #ff0000;">higher concentrations of the toxic metal Cd.</span> Significant differences were also detected for some other (e.g. minerals and vitamins) compounds&#8230;&#8221;<br />
