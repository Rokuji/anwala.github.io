It has been an up-and-down ride.</p><p>The extended period of time taken to mull over his future shows that Cook has made the right decision for him. He will be incredibly comfortable with what lies ahead. That is likely to be scoring many more runs for England.</p><ul><li><a href="/sport/cricket/38554063">Read more from Aggers</a></li></ul><h3 class="sp-story-body__cross-head">Is Root a certainty?</h3><p>Root, who was Cook's vice-captain, made his England debut against India in 2012 and has won 53 caps.</p><p>The 26-year-old Yorkshireman is the overwhelming favourite to replace Cook, but England head coach Bayliss indicated last week that all options would be looked at.</p><p>Speaking to BBC Sport two weeks ago, Root said it was "hard to say" how he would deal with becoming England captain.</p><p>"I've got a lot of experience in Test cricket and it's one of those things that you learn on the job," he added.</p><p>Root said he would like Cook to remain in the Test team as "whether as captain or not he will be a massive leader within the dressing room".</p><p>Former England off-spinner Graeme Swann has said he is "not convinced" appointing Root would be the right decision.</p><p>"Root is the outstanding candidate, but you wouldn't want it to be a case of making your best player captain, only for it to backfire on you later," said Swann.</p><p>"I want him to concentrate on being the best player we have ever had, rather than having his talent curbed by the pressures of captaincy."</p><h3 class="sp-story-body__cross-head">Ups and downs</h3><figure class="sp-media-asset "><div class="sp-media-asset__body media">

at one point the left-hander went almost two years without a Test century - and England struggling, he came under immense pressure in the summer of 2014.</p><p>After defeats by Sri Lanka at Headingley and India at Lord's, the likes of Vaughan and Alec Stewart, another former England captain, called for Cook to resign.</p><p>Cook, who later admitted he came close to quitting, revived his tenure with 95 against India at Southampton, an innings that set England on the way to a 3-1 series win.</p><p>Although he was sacked as one-day captain in late 2014, he regained the Ashes in the summer of 2015 and led England to a 2-1 series victory in South Africa, the world's number one team at the time.</p><p>In the summer of 2016, he became the first England batsman to reach 10,000 Test runs and <a class="sp-story-body__external-link" href="http://www.dailymail.co.uk/sport/cricket/article-3441121/Alastair-Cook-going-step-England-captain-Ashes-finally-feel-comfortable-role.html">spoke of his desire to lead on the Ashes tour of 2017-18.<i class="sp-story-body__external-link-icon gelicon gelicon--external" aria-hidden="true"></i></a></p><p>However, he began speculation over his future with his comments before the India series.</p><p>The four defeats took him to 22 as captain, an England record, while eight Test losses in 2016 equals their worst calendar year.</p><h3 class="sp-story-body__cross-head">What they said</h3><p>Cook's mentor <a href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p04s1xdn">Gooch,</a> another former England captain, said he had told Cook to stay on.</p><p>"This type of sportsman only comes once in a generation, maybe less.
