    <h1 class="post-title">Bangladesh refuses Rohingya fleeing 'ethnic cleansing'</h1>
    <p class="article-heading-des">Eight boats carrying Rohingya refugees have been turned away by Bangladesh as thousands amass on the border.</p>
    <div class="article-heading-author-wrap">
      <div class="article-heading-author-img">
        <div class="article-heading-author-name">
          <div class="article-duration"><time class="timeagofunction" datetime="Mon Nov 28 2016 12:15:27 GMT+0000">28 Nov 2016 12:15 GMT</time></div>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="article-readToMe-share-block" id="article-top-share-icon">
      <ul></ul>
    </div>
  </div>
</div></div></div></div></div></div><div></div></div></div></div><div><div><div><div style="width:100%"><div><div class="row article-main-content-wrapper"><div><div><div><div style="width:100%"><div><div class="col-md-8 article-main-content"><div><div style=";border-style:none"><article id="main-article-block" class="main-article-block clear-fix"><div class="main-article-body"><figure class="main-article-mediaCaption"><div class="main-article-media"><img class="img-responsive main-article-media-img lazy" data-src="/mritems/imagecache/mbdxxlarge/mritems/Images/2016/11/28/a757537d26ab4185880e4efb0de32c2b_18.jpg" alt="Bangladesh refuses Rohingya fleeing 'ethnic cleansing'" title="Bangladesh refuses Rohingya fleeing 'ethnic cleansing'"></div><figcaption>Thousands of Rohingya refugees have crossed into Bangladesh by boat in recent weeks [File: Mohammad Ponir Hossain/Reuters]</figcaption></figure><div id="inpage-more-on-block" class="inpage-auto-embed hidden-xs"></div>
    <div class="article-p-wrapper" id="body-200771816342556199"><p>Multiple boats packed with Rohingya refugees fleeing violence in Myanmar have been turned back by Bangladesh border guards despite appeals by the country's opposition to provide shelter to the persecuted Muslim minority.</p>
<div data-embed-type="Brightcove" data-embed-id="5218920792001">

</div>
<p><!-- PAGELOADEDSUCCESSFULLY--></p>
<p>Thousands of desperate Rohingya from Myanmar's western Rakhine state have flooded over the border into Bangladesh in the past week, bringing with them horrifying claims of gang rape, torture and murder at the hands of Myanmar's security forces.</p>
<p>Eight boats attempting to cross the Naf River separating Rakhine from southern Bangladesh were pushed back on Monday after six were refused entry on Sunday, <span>Colonel Abuzar Al Zahid, the </span>head of the border guards in the Bangladeshi frontier town of Teknaf,  told AFP</p>
<p>"There were 12 to 13 Rohingya in each of the boats," Zahid said.</p>
<p>Dhaka says thousands more are massed on the border, but has refused urgent international appeals to let them in, instead calling on Myanmar to do more to stop people fleeing.</p>
<p><span>In the past two weeks, Bangladeshi border guards have prevented more than 1,000 Rohingya, including many women and children, from entering the country by boat, officials told AFP.</span></p>
<div>
<div class="imagecontainer item" data-image-url="/mritems/Images/2016/11/28/40457851c2c24956b0c47f91b6da3f7c_18.jpg">
<table class="image" border="0">
<tbody>
<tr>
<td><img src="/mritems/Images/2016/11/28/40457851c2c24956b0c47f91b6da3f7c_18.jpg" border="0"></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td class="caption">Mass protests by opposition groups and religious movements have called on Bangladesh to accept Rohingya [Abir Abdullah/EPA]</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
</div>
</div>
<p>Bangladesh's main opposition leader Khaleda Zia late on Sunday joined a growing chorus of political parties and religious groups in the Muslim majority country calling for the Rohingya to be given shelter.</p>
<h2>'Torched our home'</h2>
<p>At least 30,000 have been internally displaced in Rakhine and many have tried to reach Bangladesh over the past month despite heightened border patrols, seeking refuge in Rohingya camps across the Bangladeshi border. </p>
<p class="ReadMoreContentSeparator"><a href="http://www.aljazeera.com/indepth/features/2016/11/myanmar-rohingya-braced-attacks-161107124735591.html"> READ MORE: Myanmar's Rohingya brace for more attacks in Rakhine </a></p>
<p>Samira Akhter told AFP by phone that she reached an unofficial refugee camp in Bangladesh on Monday, after fleeing her village in Rakhine state with her three children and 49 others.</p>
<p><span>"The military killed my husband and torched our home. I fled to a hill along with my three children and neighbours. We hid there for a week," said Akhter, 27.</span></p>
<p>Dudu Mia, a Rohingya leader in the camp, said at least 1,338 had arrived in the community since mid October.</p>
<div>
<div class="imagecontainer" data-image-url="/mritems/Images/2015/9/20/9092c8160ac341cf8595d7551b94cd0a_6.png">
<table class="in-article-item image width330" border="0">
<tbody>
<tr>
<td><a href="http://www.aljazeera.com/curated/" target="_blank"><img src="/mritems/Images/2015/9/20/9092c8160ac341cf8595d7551b94cd0a_6.png" border="0"></a></td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
</div>
</div>
<p>Violence in Rakhine - home to the stateless ethnic group loathed by many of Myanmar's Buddhist majority - surged in the past month after security forces poured into the area following a series of attacks on police posts blamed on local fighters.</p>
<p>A UN official said last week that Myanmar is <a href="http://www.aljazeera.com/news/2016/11/rohingya-face-myanmar-ethnic-cleansing-official-161125065731036.html">engaged in "ethnic cleansing"</a> of Rohingya Muslims, as reports emerged of troops shooting at villagers as they tried to flee.</p>
<p>Earlier this month, Human Rights Watch condemned Myanmar's torching of three Rohingya villages.</p>
<p>The rights group urged the UN to investigate the destruction of 430 buildings in the northern Maungdaw district between October 22 and November 10. </p>
<p>But Myanmar's new civilian government, led by Nobel Peace Prize winner Aung San Suu Kyi, has rejected the allegations.</p></div>
