id="post-156437557510" class="post post-type-text"><div class="post-content"><h3><a href="http://pr.aljazeera.com/post/156437557510/eu-chief-brexit-negotiator-says-technically">EU Chief Brexit Negotiator says &ldquo;technically impossible&rdquo; for new UK -EU &nbsp;trade deal within two years</a></h3><div class="post-date">
                                        January 27, 2017
                                    </div><!-- .date --><figure class="tmblr-full" data-orig-height="1080" data-orig-width="1920"><img src="http://78.media.tumblr.com/b1984f1469756f861e6581788b487d16/tumblr_inline_okfmi0RGTg1rwl04r_500.jpg" data-orig-height="1080" data-orig-width="1920" width="500" height="281" alt="image" /></figure><p>Guy Verhofstadt MEP, the European Parliament&rsquo;s chief Brexit negotiator,
speaks with <i>UpFront</i>:</p><ul><li>Says a &lsquo;Little Englander&rsquo;
mentality could be a &ldquo;good explanation&rdquo; for why Brits voted for Brexit<br /></li><li>Declares it &ldquo;technically
impossible&rdquo; for the UK to have a new trade deal in place with the EU in two years&rsquo;
time<br /></li><li>&nbsp;Suggests it&rsquo;s &ldquo;always
possible&rdquo; that the UK might rejoin the EU in the future and it could be a
&ldquo;little bit faster a process&rdquo;<br /></li><li>Describes Brexit and other
political challenges to the EU - from Trump to Putin - as an &ldquo;existential
threat&rdquo;</li></ul><p><b>(Washington, D.C. &ndash; 27<sup>th</sup>, January)</b> - In an interview with Al
Jazeera English&rsquo;s current affairs show, <i>UpFront</i>, Guy Verhofstadt, the
European Parliament&rsquo;s chief Brexit negotiator, speaks about Brexit, Britain and
the challenges facing the European Union (EU).</p><p>&nbsp;Asked about which issue drove 52% of Britons to vote for Brexit in
June 2016, Verhofstadt replied:&nbsp;&ldquo;Mainly the migration. It&rsquo;s very clear.&rdquo;</p><p>When asked by&nbsp;<i>UpFront</i>&nbsp;host Mehdi Hasan if
xenophobia and specifically a <i>Little
Englander</i> mentality explained the Brexit vote, the MEP and former Belgian
prime minister responded, &ldquo;That&rsquo;s maybe a good explanation&rdquo;.</p><p>During the interview, Verhofstadt was also asked whether it would
be possible for the UK government to negotiate both Brexit <i>and</i> a new trade deal with the EU by 2019.&nbsp;</p><p>&ldquo;That&rsquo;s technically impossible,&rdquo; he said, referring to the
timeline on a future trade agreement.</p><p>He also emphasized that whilst they are not looking for a punitive
exercise, the EU parliament will want a fair and generous agreement, where &ldquo;you
can never have outside the European Union a better status than as member of the
European Union&rdquo;.</p><p>Verhofstadt also gave his thoughts on whether the UK could
potentially re-enter the EU in the future under a different government. &ldquo;That
is always possible,&rdquo; he said. &ldquo;They can always reintroduce a request for
membership of the European Union.&rdquo;</p><p>&ldquo;Certainly, we have enough experience to make it a little bit a faster
process than what is normal,&rdquo; he added.</p><p>The senior MEP and former Belgian prime minister also agreed that
the numerous challenges facing the EU - the election of Donald Trump, the rise
of Putin&rsquo;s Russia, Brexit, and the fiscal and refugee crises - constitute an
&ldquo;existential threat&rdquo; to the future of the union.&nbsp;</p><p><b>Note to Editors</b><br /></p><p>&middot;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
