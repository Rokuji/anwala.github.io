                                        February 3, 2017
                                    </div><!-- .date --><figure class="tmblr-full" data-orig-height="1080" data-orig-width="1920"><img src="http://78.media.tumblr.com/35dc91fccdaeb3a30030070f35be55eb/tumblr_inline_okshlwpwJY1rwl04r_500.jpg" data-orig-height="1080" data-orig-width="1920" width="500" height="281" alt="image" /></figure><ul><li>Ali Soufan says the Trump administration&rsquo;s Muslim ban is couched in
&ldquo;anti-Muslim sentiments&rdquo;<br /></li><li>Proclaims that torture is &ldquo;anti-American and will never happen&rdquo;<br /></li><li>Warns that Trump&rsquo;s travel ban &ldquo;works directly&rdquo; into ISIL&rsquo;s propaganda
narrative<br /></li></ul><p><b>(Washington, D.C. &ndash; 3<sup>rd</sup>, February)</b> - In an interview with Al
Jazeera English&rsquo;s current affairs show <i>UpFront</i>, Ali Soufan, a former FBI
agent and interrogator, said the Trump
administration&rsquo;s decision to ban citizens
from seven Muslim majority countries from entering the United States &ldquo;does not
help our national security interests&rdquo;. </p><p>Soufan
told <i>UpFront </i>host Mehdi Hasan, the ban
&ldquo;actually works directly into Al Qaeda and the so-called Islamic State&rsquo;s propaganda&rdquo;.</p><p>Asked
about the motives behind the implementation of the
ban, Soufan said, he believed &ldquo;anti-Muslim sentiments&rdquo; played
a role.</p><p>&ldquo;It
is very difficult to say that there is not an anti-Muslim sentiment in these
policies to cater to a specific base that they probably have in Washington,&rdquo; he
said. &nbsp;</p><p>During the interview, Soufan &mdash; who has interrogated Al Qaeda members without the use of torture &mdash; reacted to President Donald Trump&rsquo;s
pronouncement that torture was an effective tool in the fight against ISIL and
that the practice should be reimplemented.</p><p>&ldquo;We have so
many different investigations from the CIA, the FBI, the Department of Defense,
the Armed Services Committee, the Senate Select Committee of Intelligence and
we have a total agreement, it [torture] did
not prevent one single terrorist attack in the United States,&rdquo; he said.</p><p>Soufan
also pointed out that to bring back the practice, the president would need
Congress to overturn a law banning torture. &ldquo;That
is so anti-American and it will never happen.&rdquo;</p><p>The full show will be available through this link:<a href="https://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.aljazeera.com%2Fupfront&amp;t=N2RkYjAxZTM2NzM3MDJjMzUzODRlYTk4ZjUwNzdiYzhmNzRjNjUyNix1TEg1RGhJcw%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3ApDthwcc1nHa3dqRrCMfF3Q&amp;p=http%3A%2F%2Fpr.aljazeera.com%2Fpost%2F156750610455%2Fformer-fbi-agent-tells-al-jazeera-muslim-ban-a&amp;m=1"> www.aljazeera.com/upfront</a></p><p><br /></p><p><b>Note to Editors</b></p><p>&middot;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
