DeVos' track record suggests that as secretary, she will do little to combat these trends. </div><div class="ad ad--epic ad--mobile" data-ad-text="show"><div data-ad-id="ad_rect_atf_02" data-ad-position="mobile" data-ad-refresh="default"><div id="ad_rect_atf_02" class="ad-ad_rect_atf_02 ad-refresh-default"></div></div></div><div class="ad ad--epic ad--tablet" data-ad-text="show"><div data-ad-id="ad_rect_atf_02" data-ad-position="tablet" data-ad-refresh="default"></div></div><div class="ad ad--epic ad--desktop" data-ad-text="show"><div data-ad-id="ad_rect_atf_02" data-ad-position="desktop" data-ad-refresh="default"></div></div><ul class="cn cn-list-hierarchical-xs cn--idx-6 cn-zoneAdContainer" data-layout="list-hierarchical-xs"></ul><div class="zn-body__paragraph">In the wake of the 1954 Brown v. Board Supreme Court decision, which famously declared that separate schools for black and white children were not equal, the federal government began -- albeit slowly -- to desegregate. This applied to schools in the South that were previously forbidden by law from educating children of different races together and to schools in the North plagued by de facto segregation. </div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">The backlash, now infamous but perhaps too familiar even today, was fierce. From Alabama Governor George Wallace's stance in the schoolhouse door and his proclamations of "segregation now, segregation forever" to Louise Day Hicks leading the Boston antibusing movement, the reaction to desegregation roiled American politics. </div><div class="el__embedded el__embedded--standard"><div class="el__storyelement--standard el__article--embed"><div class="el__article--teaseimage"><a href="/2017/01/19/opinions/betsy-devos-grizzlies-and-guns-filipovic/index.html" ><img class="media__image media__image--responsive" alt="What Betsy DeVos&amp;#39; Thus, an <a href="https://www.splcenter.org/hatewatch/2010/10/28/white-supremacist-group-backs-private-academies-mississippi" target="_blank">estimated half-million </a>white students left public schools between 1964 and 1975 to enroll in schools that were known as "segregation academies." This move to private schools was part of a larger "white flight" movement. </div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">White flight was one of the greatest demographic shifts in American history. Millions of whites nationwide moved out of cities and into racially isolated suburbs. Scholar Kevin Kruse has <a href="http://press.princeton.edu/chapters/i8043.html" target="_blank">called white flight</a> "the most successful segregationist response to the moral demands of the civil rights movement and the legal authority of the courts." The character and quality of most American schools today, like the neighborhoods in which they are found and which they shape, have a racial past.</div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">Now, American public schools are falling back into a kind of racial segregation that is reminiscent of our country before Brown. In the last 25 years, the number of severely racially isolated schools, defined as those with 0-10% white students, has <a href="https://urldefense.proofpoint.com/v2/url?u=https-3A__www.civilrightsproject.ucla.edu_research_k-2D12-2Deducation_integration-2Dand-2Ddiversity_brown-2Dat-2D62-2Dschool-2Dsegregation-2Dby-2Drace-2Dpoverty-2Dand-2Dstate_Brown-2Dat-2D62-2Dfinal-2Dcorrected-2D2.pdf&d=DwMFAg&c=W8uiIUydLnv14aAum3Oieg&r=haYvn8UTtJhMJ5UTl6vlf47BSCqAnqLmI2yjukbrr3s&m=57FX3AFAQdsKldZyOEvKY7U3KibFbyG-Fe7Fe8KDWRA&s=rmM8ZCLwfOXCWgkTcx48DLdFIkrI8CPPreKtkJTMq7g&e=" target="_blank">tripled</a>.
