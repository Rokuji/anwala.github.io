class="post-date">
                                        January 12, 2017
                                    </div><!-- .date --><figure class="tmblr-full" data-orig-height="1080" data-orig-width="1920"><img src="http://78.media.tumblr.com/24c0784ed2b5a4e2834e94c2835ec8f6/tumblr_inline_ojnq3fVBLL1rwl04r_500.jpg" data-orig-height="1080" data-orig-width="1920" width="500" height="281" alt="image" /></figure><ul><li>Says those involved with Obama&rsquo;s Syria policy &ldquo;don&rsquo;t look back
with any pride upon what Syria is today&rdquo;<br /></li><li>&nbsp;Says authorizing
use of drones was one the most &ldquo;difficult issues&rdquo; Obama
had to face<br /></li><li>Expresses concern over President-elect Donald Trump inheriting
Obama&rsquo;s military
powers, including the expanded drone program: &ldquo;It
worries me a lot&rdquo;</li></ul><p><b>Washington, D.C. &ndash; </b><b>12<sup>th</sup>, January 2017</b>&nbsp;- In an interview with Al
Jazeera English&rsquo;s current affairs show <i>UpFront</i>, Derek Chollet, former
assistant secretary of defense under
U.S. President Barack Obama,
speaks about the president&rsquo;s legacy.</p><p>&nbsp;&ldquo;Those of us who were involved in the making
of Syria policy in the last five years don&rsquo;t look with any pride upon what
Syria is today,&rdquo; Chollet told <i>UpFront </i>host Mehdi Hasan.</p><p>&nbsp;&ldquo;There are many things, as I&rsquo;ve looked back,
that the U.S. could have done differently,&rdquo; he said.</p><p>However, Chollet, who wrote
the book<i> The Long Game: How Obama Defied Washington and Redefined America&rsquo;s
Role in the World,</i> did say that the administration&rsquo;s biggest success on
Syria was reducing the
Assad government&rsquo;s stockpile of chemical
weapons.</p><p>&ldquo;I think 1,300 tonnes of chemical weapons out of Syria is good for the world,
good for us,&rdquo; he said.</p><p>On the subject of the Iran deal, Chollet
dismissed claims that the president crafted his Syria policies to accommodate the Iranian government and ensure the Iran deal
remains intact.</p><p>&ldquo;I don&rsquo;t see that, in any way, connected
to any of the policy decisions on Syria,&rdquo; he said.</p><p>During the interview, Chollet was also asked
about President Obama&rsquo;s use of drones.</p><p>&ldquo;No question that the use of drones has been
one of the more controversial and difficult issues that President Obama&rsquo;s had
to face,&rdquo; he said.</p><p>&ldquo;He&rsquo;s used them quite effectively,&rdquo; he added.</p><p>When asked further if he was worried about
President-elect Donald Trump inheriting Obama&rsquo;s military powers, including the
expanded drone program, he replied: &ldquo;It worries me a lot.&rdquo;</p><p><b>Note to Editors</b><br /></p><p>&middot;
