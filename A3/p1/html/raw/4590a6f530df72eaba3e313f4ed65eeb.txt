	<span class="caption-box pull-left" style="float: left; width: 620px;"><img alt="The Ministry of Health would investigate doctors and healthcare facilities highlighted in an Al-Jazeera documentary on baby-selling syndicates. — Reuters file pic" height="413" src="http://www.themalaymailonline.com/uploads/articles/2014-05/babies.jpeg" style="float: left;" width="620"><span class="img-caption">The Ministry of Health would investigate doctors and healthcare facilities highlighted in an Al-Jazeera documentary on baby-selling syndicates. — Reuters file pic</span></span>KUALA LUMPUR, Nov 27 — The Ministry of Health announced yesterday it has kicked off investigation towards doctors and healthcare facilities highlighted in an Al Jazeera documentary on baby-selling syndicates.</p>
<p>
	Based on the results of the investigation, the doctors will face disciplinary actions by the Malaysian Medical Council which may lead to deregistration from the Medical Register under the Medical Act 1971, while the centres may have their certificate or license revoked.</p>
<div id="misc-ads" style="margin: 10px;"></div><p>
	“On that note, the Ministry has already initiated an investigation specifically towards the doctors and healthcare facilities highlighted in this documentary.</p>
<p>
	“Depending on the result of the investigations, actions will be taken accordingly towards the doctors and healthcare facilities,” Health Minister Datuk Dr S Subramaniam said in a statement.</p>
<p>
	According to Subramaniam, all medical practitioners must observe proper standards of personal behaviour to keep the dignity of the profession, and involvement in human trafficking may amount to derogatory conduct to the profession’s reputation.</p>
<p>
	He also explained that all health facilities and services are controlled and regulated under the Private Healthcare Facilities and Services Act 1998 and its subsidiaries, and they must ensure all practices are legal.</p>
<p>
	In the episode aired this week, Al Jazeera journalist Chan Tau Chou exposed the ease with which babies could allegedly be purchased in Malaysia.</p>
<p>
	The show also alleged that traffickers offered catalogues of pregnant women for customers to browse when shopping for babies.</p>
<p>
	Posing as potential baby buyers, the Al Jazeera team discovered that traffickers in Malaysia housed as many as 78 pregnant Indonesian women.</p>
<p>
	The team also gathered video evidence of doctors who openly offer to help procure falsified birth documents for babies who have been bought, allegedly with the help of officials working in the National Registration Department.</p>
<p>
	“At the same time, the Ministry is urging the public to help curb this activity, by being more alert and willing to come forward with the relevant information, for further action by the relevant agencies,” Subramaniam added.</p>
<p>
	“Thus, the participation of the community to be hand-in-hand with the Ministry in safeguarding the patients’ safety and the quality of our healthcare system is highly appreciated to assist in putting a stop to this dissolute activity.”</p>
